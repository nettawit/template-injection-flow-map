Template Injection — What We Tested

Manual testing of Aria's ability to personalize Wix templates for different business types. We ran two rounds for each template — once with a basic ask, and once with an explicit instruction to preserve the template's style and layout.

1

Open a template

Manually selected templates across different categories — fitness, fashion, marketing, art, SaaS. Each has its own visual identity.

2

Round 1 — basic ask

Asked Aria: "Change the content and images to fit [business type]." No additional guidance.

3

Round 2 — with style anchor

Same ask, adding: "…and keep the same style and layout as the template." Compared both results.

The question
Does telling Aria to preserve the template's visual style produce a meaningfully better result — and what else breaks regardless?

Conclusions

What we learned

Core finding

Adding "keep the same style and layout as the template" to the injection prompt produces significantly better results. Content feels like it belongs in the template — not forced into it.

What works well

  • Text quality — Aria generates relevant, on-brand copy.
  • Image consistency — injected images feel thematically right.
  • Similar pivots — when business type is close to template, it works.

What still breaks

  • Incomplete injection — some sections skipped (hero vs nav).
  • App widget images — Gallery, Stores, Bookings not updated.
  • Big pivots — very different business types don't adapt well.

Recommendation

Make style + layout preservation a default system instruction in every injection prompt. Already proven to improve results — low effort, high impact.

Summary

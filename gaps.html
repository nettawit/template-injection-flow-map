Harmony · Template Injection

Product Gaps & Research Findings

A summary of what's working, what's not, and where we need to focus next.

Wix Research
July 2026
Internal
Context

What we tested

We mapped Before / After results from template injection across a range of business types and scenarios.

🗂️
Use case
User selects a template, Aria collects business info, then injects personalized content into the template.
🔬
What we looked at
Text content, images, layout stability, brand alignment, and results across different business types.
📐
Scenarios tested
Same business type with minor detail changes. Different business type, same template. With and without brand & style instructions.
Findings — What works

The good news

Before injection — original template
The original EPS Marketing template — clean layout, template-native copy, consistent visual style.
  • Text content quality is good. Aria generates relevant, on-brand copy that fits the business type.
  • Images come from the same visual family. The injected images are thematically consistent — they don't feel random.
  • Small changes work well. When only details change (name, tagline, city), the result is accurate and polished.
Gap 1

Text content breaks the layout

Aria generates text that's semantically correct, but doesn't account for character limits or layout constraints.

Original template
Layout holds with short, template-native copy. Text fits within designed containers. Hero headline is concise.
After injection
Business name injected into the nav ("EPS MARKETING") but the hero headline "Your Brand's Key to Better Marketing" wasn't updated — showing partial, inconsistent injection.
Gap 2

Images aren't injected into apps

Image replacement works on standard page sections, but Wix app widgets (galleries, stores, bookings) are not updated during injection.

Tech template — Before injection
MicroLink SaaS template with OtherSync dashboard — standard Wix sections and app widgets visible. Aria is connected and ready to personalize.
After injection — apps untouched
🖼️ Gallery, Stores, and Bookings app widgets keep their original placeholder images — even after full injection.
Standard sections get new images. App widget images are not part of the injection scope — they remain unchanged and break the personalized experience.
Why this matters
App widgets (Gallery, Stores, Bookings) are often the most prominent visual areas on the page. When they keep generic images, the site feels half-personalized.
Gap 3

Bigger pivots break the result

When the user's business type differs significantly from the template's original intent, injection results degrade — copy doesn't fit the context and structure becomes awkward.

Similar business type — works well
☕ → 🥐
Coffee shop → Bakery
Small pivot: same lifestyle, similar audience, close visual expectations
When the business type is adjacent to the template's original purpose, the injection adapts well. Sections, images, and tone remain coherent.
Different business type — breaks
☕ → 🏋️
Coffee shop template → Gym
Big pivot: forced copy, jarring images, structure no longer fits
Bigger pivots produce a jarring result — the template structure no longer fits the business, and injected content feels forced into the wrong container.

Key Finding

Brand & style instructions improve results

When Aria includes an explicit instruction to maintain the template's original visual brand and style, the output is noticeably more aligned — especially for text tone and image selection.

Without brand/style instruction
Aria injects content without anchoring to the template's visual identity. Business name appears in the nav, but the hero and other sections aren't updated to reflect the brand.
With brand/style instruction
When Aria is instructed to preserve the template's visual brand, the injected content feels like a natural extension of the original design. Style remains consistent throughout.
Recommendation
Make brand & style preservation a default system instruction in every injection prompt — not an optional addition. This is low-effort, high-impact.

What's next

Next steps

1
Add character-limit awareness to injection
Pass container max-char limits as part of the injection context so Aria generates text that respects layout constraints.
2
Extend image injection to Wix app widgets
Gallery, Stores, and Bookings widgets need a separate injection path. Coordinate with app teams to expose image update APIs during personalization.
3
Make brand & style preservation a default
Include a brand/style instruction in every injection prompt by default. Already proven to improve output quality — low effort, high impact.
4
Research template–business type matching
Filter or rank templates by business type similarity to reduce the frequency of large pivots before injection even begins.
